When Should You Trim Your Trees?

Most trees benefit from regular trimming every 3–5 years. However, dead or crossing branches should be removed as soon as they're identified — waiting increases the risk of disease spread and structural failure.

The best time for pruning most Idaho species is late winter while trees are dormant, minimizing stress and reducing exposure to insects and disease.

Signs Your Tree May Be Hazardous

  • Leaning more than 15° from vertical
  • Large dead branches in the canopy
  • Fungal growth or mushrooms at the base
  • Cracks or splits in major limbs
  • Hollowing or decay near the trunk
